Food Control and Public Safety

What is Food Control?

Food control refers to the measures and regulations applied to ensure that food is safe, hygienic, nutritious, and suitable for human consumption. It plays a vital role in protecting public health.

Why Food Control is Important

Effective food control helps prevent food-borne illnesses, ensures quality standards, and builds consumer confidence in food products.

Unsafe food can cause serious health problems and outbreaks of disease.

Key Objectives of Food Control

  • Ensure food hygiene and cleanliness
  • Prevent contamination and adulteration
  • Maintain nutritional quality
  • Protect consumer health
